About

Reading genomes, decoding disease.

I am a Principal Research Scientist at the Korea Research Institute of Bioscience and Biotechnology (KRIBB), based in the Korea Bioinformation Center (KOBIC). My lab connects genome, epigenome, and transcriptome data with clinical phenotypes — and builds the public infrastructure that makes such analyses reproducible at national scale.

Recent work resolves structural variation in telomere-aware Korean draft genomes (NAR, 2025), maps DNA methylation and recurrence in stage-III Korean colorectal cancer (BMB Reports & PLOS ONE, 2024–2025), characterizes the neoepitope landscape shaped by MHC II immunogenicity (Nature Genetics, 2023), and identifies Mendelian stroke variants in 15,548 healthy Koreans (Journal of Stroke, 2025).

Over two decades I have helped build national-scale bio-data infrastructure — from the HUGO Pan-Asian SNP Consortium (Science, 2009) to KRIBB's Korean Nucleotide Archive (KoNA) and the Korean Transposable Element Database (KTED) launched in 2024.

Research Themes

What I work on, at a glance.

Five intertwined threads run through the lab — each one anchored to ongoing Korean cohorts and reflected in the highlighted papers below.

Korean Population Genomics

Reference panels, ethnic-specific variants, and structural-variation catalogs — from the HUGO Pan-Asian SNP project to telomere-aware Korean draft genomes.

NAR 2025 · EMM 2022 · Science 2009

Cancer Multi-Omics

Integrating genome, methylome, and transcriptome data from Korean colorectal- and lung-cancer cohorts to surface clinically actionable biomarkers and neoepitopes.

Nature Genetics 2023 · Gastroenterology 2021 · BMB Reports 2024

Structural Variants & Genome Assembly

Benchmarking long-read SV callers and resolving complex regions — centromeres, telomeres, transposable elements — in massive whole-genome cohorts.

NAR 2025 · BMC Genomics 2024 · Bioinform. Adv. 2024 (KTED)

Rare-Disease Variant Discovery

Pinpointing pathogenic variants in Korean families with Lennox–Gastaut syndrome, hereditary spastic paraplegia, and Mendelian stroke phenotypes.

J Stroke 2025 · Frontiers Genet. 2021 · Genomics 2021

Public Bio-Data Infrastructure

Designing and operating national repositories so the biology can be shared and reused — KoNA for nucleotide submissions, KTED for transposable elements, CanISO for canine genomics, and KOBIC's Bio Data Station behind them.

GPB 2024 (KoNA) · Bioinform. Adv. 2024 (KTED) · BMC Genomics 2023 (CanISO)
