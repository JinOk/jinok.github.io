About

Reading genomes, decoding disease.

I am a Principal Research Scientist at the Korea Research Institute of Bioscience and Biotechnology (KRIBB), based in the Korea Bioinformation Center (KOBIC). My research builds computational frameworks that connect genome sequence, epigenome, and transcriptome data with clinical phenotypes — most recently in colorectal cancer, lung cancer, and rare neurogenetic disorders in Korean populations.

Across two decades I have helped build national-scale bio-data infrastructure, from the early HUGO Pan-Asian SNP project to today's population-scale whole-genome and methylome cohorts. I am especially interested in interpretable multi-omics analysis that survives the jump from a discovery cohort to a clinic.
